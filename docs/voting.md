# Voting / 投票规则

[中文](#中文) | [English](#english)

---

## 中文

### 1. 文档目的

本文件用于定义 AntiGEO 的正式投票规则、投票前提、结果处理方式与相关注意事项。它说明哪些提案可以进入正式投票，投票应解决什么问题，以及投票结果如何影响注册表中的 `watchlist`、`restricted` 与 `blocked`。

### 2. 投票的作用

AntiGEO 的投票不是为了表达品牌好恶，也不是为了对现实世界主体作终局裁定。投票的作用是：

- 在完成基本审核后
- 基于现有证据与定义
- 对某个提案应对应何种注册表状态形成社区判断

换言之，投票处理的是注册表中的风险状态，而不是对现实世界主体作价值判断或法律结论。

### 3. 哪些提案可以进入正式投票

提案进入正式投票，至少应满足以下前提：

- 提案信息基本完整
- 有足够的可复核证据
- 已经过基本审核
- 与 `definition.md` 和 `governance.md` 一致
- 不属于明显恶意、重复、骚扰式或证据不足的提案

没有足够可复核证据的提案，不应进入正式投票。若关键事实仍不清楚，社区应优先补充审核，而不是过早发起投票。

### 4. 投票可处理的事项

正式投票至少可以处理以下事项：

- 是否将对象加入 `watchlist`
- 是否将状态从 `watchlist` 升级到 `restricted`
- 是否将状态从 `restricted` 升级到 `blocked`
- 是否降级状态
- 是否从注册表中移除
- 是否维持原状态
- 是否接受申诉后的状态调整

### 5. 投票时应遵循的原则

社区在投票时，应遵循以下原则：

- 证据优先
- 谨慎认定
- 避免过度推断
- 状态强度应与证据强度匹配
- 区分“值得关注”与“足以更高强度处理”
- 避免品牌偏好、竞争立场或情绪化判断

对于更高强度的状态，尤其应更加谨慎。证据不足以支持更高强度状态时，不应仅因主观风险感受而提高处理等级。

### 6. 投票结果的处理

投票结果应形成明确的状态结论。可选结果通常可以包括：

- 维持
- 加入
- 升级
- 降级
- 移除

投票结果应记录在案，并保留必要历史。投票结果不是永久性的，后续仍可因新证据、事实更正、申诉成立或复审结论而调整。

### 7. 不发起或中止投票的情况

在以下常见情形下，社区可以不发起、延后、中止或驳回投票：

- 证据明显不足
- 提案信息严重缺失
- 提案明显不符合定义范围
- 存在恶意提交迹象
- 大量关键事实仍待核实
- 提案与现有已处理提案高度重复且无新增内容

若投票已启动但后续发现基础材料存在明显缺口，社区也可以选择中止投票并返回补充审核。

### 8. 申诉与复审中的投票

申诉或复审可以触发新的投票，但新投票不自动推翻旧结果。社区应基于新增材料、事实更正或更完整的上下文重新判断。

在申诉与复审场景下，投票结果仍可以是：

- 维持
- 升级
- 降级
- 移除

### 9. 与其它文档的关系

各文档分工如下：

- `definition.md`：负责界定范围
- `governance.md`：负责总体治理框架
- `evidence-policy.md`：负责证据规范
- `voting.md`：负责正式投票规则
- `appeals.md`：将负责申诉流程细节

当具体规则出现交叉时，应以 `governance.md` 作为总体治理框架，并结合本文件与对应专题文档共同理解。

### 10. 早期项目说明

AntiGEO 仍处于早期阶段，投票细则未来可能随着社区经验而通过公开方式修订。此类修订应保持透明、克制与可审计，并继续强调证据优先、谨慎认定与防止滥用。

本投票规则未来可通过社区公开修订。

## English

### 1. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the AntiGEO rules for formal voting, including voting prerequisites, result handling, and related considerations. It explains which proposals are eligible for voting, what formal voting is meant to resolve, and how voting outcomes affect `watchlist`, `restricted`, and `blocked` in the registry.

### 2. The Function of Voting

AntiGEO voting is not a mechanism for expressing brand preference, nor is it a way to make a final determination about a real-world entity. The purpose of voting is:

- After basic review has been completed
- Based on the available evidence and definitions
- To form a community judgment about which registry status, if any, should apply to a proposal

In other words, voting addresses risk status in the registry, not value judgments or legal conclusions about a real-world entity.

### 3. Which Proposals Are Eligible for Formal Voting

A proposal should meet at least the following prerequisites before entering formal voting:

- The proposal information is basically complete
- There is sufficient verifiable evidence
- Basic review has already taken place
- The proposal is consistent with `definition.md` and `governance.md`
- It is not plainly malicious, duplicative, harassing, or evidentially insufficient

Any proposal without sufficient verifiable evidence should not proceed to formal voting. If key facts remain unclear, the community should prioritize further review rather than moving to a vote too early.

### 4. Matters That Formal Voting May Resolve

Formal voting may resolve at least the following:

- Whether to add an entity to `watchlist`
- Whether to upgrade a status from `watchlist` to `restricted`
- Whether to upgrade a status from `restricted` to `blocked`
- Whether to downgrade a status
- Whether to remove an entity from the registry
- Whether to maintain the current status
- Whether to accept a status adjustment after an appeal

### 5. Principles to Follow During Voting

The community should follow these principles during voting:

- Evidence first
- Cautious determination
- Avoid over-inference
- Evidence strength should match status severity
- Distinguish between a matter worth watching and one that justifies stronger treatment
- Avoid voting based on brand preference, competitive motives, or emotion

Higher-severity statuses require greater caution. Where the evidence does not support a stronger status, the community should not elevate the outcome based only on subjective risk perception.

### 6. Handling of Voting Results

Voting outcomes should produce a clear status conclusion. Available outcomes may include:

- Maintain
- Add
- Upgrade
- Downgrade
- Remove

The outcome should be recorded in a transparent and auditable record, with necessary history preserved. A voting result is not permanent and may later be adjusted due to new evidence, factual correction, a successful appeal, or re-review.

### 7. When a Vote May Be Declined, Deferred, Suspended, or Rejected

The community may decline to start, defer, suspend, or reject a vote in situations such as:

- The evidence is plainly insufficient
- The proposal is seriously incomplete
- The proposal is clearly outside the defined scope
- There are signs of malicious submission
- Too many key facts remain unresolved
- The proposal substantially duplicates an already handled proposal without meaningful new content

If a vote has already started but significant gaps in the record are later identified, the community may suspend the vote and return the matter for further review.

### 8. Appeals and Re-Review Voting

Appeals or re-review may trigger a new vote, but a new vote does not automatically override the previous result. The community should reassess the matter based on new materials, factual corrections, or a more complete context.

In appeals and re-review cases, outcomes may still include:

- Maintain
- Upgrade
- Downgrade
- Remove

### 9. Relationship to Other Documents

The documents are divided as follows:

- `definition.md`: defines scope
- `governance.md`: provides the overall governance framework
- `evidence-policy.md`: defines evidence standards
- `voting.md`: defines the rules for formal voting
- `appeals.md`: will define the details of the appeals process

Where rules overlap, `governance.md` should be treated as the overall governance framework and read together with this document and the relevant topic-specific document.

### 10. Early-Stage Project Note

AntiGEO is still at an early stage, and the details of voting may be revised publicly as the community gains practical experience. Such revisions should remain transparent, restrained, and auditable, while continuing to emphasize evidence-first review, cautious determination, and abuse prevention.

These voting rules may be revised in the future through an open community process.
