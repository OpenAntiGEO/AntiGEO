# Definition / 定义

## 中文

### 1. 文档目的

本文件用于定义 AntiGEO 的核心术语、适用范围、边界与判断基础。它为证据提交、社区审核、投票与申诉提供共同参照，并作为 `evidence-policy`、`governance` 与 `voting` 等文档的基础定义文件。

### 2. 什么是 GEO 操纵风险（GEO manipulation risk）

在 AntiGEO 语境下，**GEO 操纵风险**是指某一对象通过可规模化、可复制、可组织化的方法，影响 AI 搜索、AI 摘要、答案引擎、检索增强系统、智能体等下游系统的来源选择、排序、曝光或呈现，从而造成失真曝光、品牌偏置、竞争压制或用户误导的风险。AntiGEO 对风险识别特别关注三个核心维度：**系统性**、**规模化**、**可复核**。

AntiGEO 关注的是**系统性操纵风险**，而不是单篇低质量内容、个别异常页面或一般性的内容优化行为本身。AntiGEO 记录的是风险及其证据基础，不构成终局定罪，也不等同于法律、监管或平台执法结论。

### 3. AntiGEO 关注的对象

AntiGEO 关注的对象可以包括但不限于：

- 品牌
- 公司或组织
- 域名
- 内容网络
- 站群
- 代理发布网络
- 其它可识别的相关实体

是否纳入 registry，不取决于对象类别本身，而取决于是否存在与 GEO manipulation risk 相关的可复核证据。

### 4. 常见风险行为

AntiGEO 当前关注的风险行为，可能包括但不限于以下模式：

- 伪媒体网络
- 伪评测网络
- 伪专家背书
- 批量化模板内容投放
- 为 AI 检索或答案系统专门构造的大规模操纵型页面
- 多站点重复或轻改写内容网络
- 以影响 AI 回答为目标的结构化品牌植入
- 其它可复核的系统性操纵模式

这些示例仅用于说明常见模式，本身不自动构成纳入 registry 的充分理由。

上述行为是否构成风险，应结合证据、重复性、规模化程度和组织化特征综合判断，而不应仅依据表面风格或主观印象作出结论。

### 5. 不在 AntiGEO 当前范围内的内容

以下内容通常不在 AntiGEO 当前关注范围内，除非另有证据显示其已形成可复核的系统性操纵模式：

- 正常官网内容
- 正常 SEO
- 合法 PR
- 明确标注的广告
- 普通用户评价
- 单篇低质量文章但未形成系统性操纵
- 单纯质量差但无证据显示存在组织化操纵的内容

AntiGEO 不将一般性的内容质量问题、市场传播活动或正常品牌建设行为直接等同于 GEO manipulation risk。

### 6. 风险认定原则

社区在审核、投票、申诉与复审时，应遵循以下原则：

- 证据优先
- 可复核
- 谨慎认定
- 重视系统性和重复性
- 避免仅凭主观印象或品牌偏好判断
- 允许申诉与复审

在具体判断中，社区应特别关注三个核心维度：**系统性**、**规模化**、**可复核**。缺乏足够证据时，不应作出过强结论。

### 7. 与状态模型的关系

本定义文档仅负责说明 AntiGEO 的范围、边界与基础术语。某一对象是否进入 `watchlist`、`restricted` 或 `blocked`，应由后续的证据提交、社区审核、投票与申诉程序决定。

换言之，定义文档回答“什么属于 AntiGEO 关注的风险”；具体状态决定回答“现有证据支持达到何种风险级别”。

### 8. 非目标

AntiGEO 当前不以以下事项为目标：

- 不对产品优劣作价值判断
- 不裁定单条内容真伪
- 不替代法律或监管结论
- 不对搜索结果质量排名本身提供统一标准
- 不直接规定各类下游工具必须采用何种执行策略

下游系统可以参考 AntiGEO registry，但是否采纳、如何执行，以及执行强度如何，应由各下游系统自行决定。

本定义文档未来可通过社区治理程序修订。

## English

### 1. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the core terms, scope, boundaries, and interpretive basis of AntiGEO. It provides a shared reference for evidence submission, community review, voting, and appeals, and serves as a foundation for documents such as `evidence-policy`, `governance`, and `voting`.

### 2. What GEO Manipulation Risk Means

Within AntiGEO, **GEO manipulation risk** refers to the risk that an entity uses scalable, repeatable, or organized methods to influence source selection, ranking, exposure, or presentation in AI search, AI summaries, answer engines, retrieval-augmented systems, agents, or similar downstream systems, in ways that may produce distorted exposure, brand bias, competitive suppression, or user misdirection.

AntiGEO is concerned with **systemic manipulation risk**, not with a single low-quality page, an isolated abnormal result, or ordinary content optimization by itself. AntiGEO records risk and its evidentiary basis; it does not constitute a final determination, and it should not be treated as a legal, regulatory, or platform enforcement conclusion.

### 3. Objects Within AntiGEO Scope

Objects within AntiGEO scope may include, but are not limited to:

- Brands
- Companies or organizations
- Domains
- Content networks
- Site clusters
- Proxy publishing networks
- Other identifiable related entities

Whether something is included in the registry depends not on its category alone, but on whether there is verifiable evidence relevant to GEO manipulation risk.

### 4. Common Risk Behaviors

Common patterns of concern may include, for example:

- Pseudo-media networks
- Pseudo-review networks
- False or manufactured expert endorsement
- Large-scale templated content deployment
- Large-scale manipulative pages constructed specifically for AI retrieval or answer systems
- Multi-site duplicate or lightly rewritten content networks
- Structured brand insertion intended to influence AI-generated answers
- Other verifiable patterns of systemic manipulation

These examples are illustrative only and do not, by themselves, provide a sufficient basis for inclusion in the registry.

Whether such conduct rises to the level of risk should be assessed in light of the evidence, repetition, scale, and organized characteristics involved, rather than inferred from style alone or from subjective impressions.

### 5. Content Outside AntiGEO's Current Scope

The following are generally outside AntiGEO's current scope unless additional evidence shows a verifiable pattern of systemic manipulation:

- Normal official website content
- Ordinary SEO
- Legitimate PR
- Clearly disclosed advertising
- Ordinary user reviews
- A single low-quality article without systemic manipulation
- Content that is merely poor in quality without evidence of organized manipulation

AntiGEO does not treat ordinary quality problems, general marketing activity, or normal brand-building efforts as GEO manipulation risk by default.

### 6. Principles for Risk Determination

Community review, voting, and appeals should follow these principles:

- Evidence first
- Verifiable evidence
- Cautious determination
- Attention to systemic and repeated patterns
- Avoid judgments based only on subjective impressions or brand preference
- Allow review and appeals

In practice, the community should pay particular attention to three core dimensions: **systemic pattern**, **scale**, and **verifiability**. Where evidence is insufficient, the project should avoid strong conclusions.

### 7. Relationship to the Status Model

This definition document is limited to defining scope, boundaries, and core terms. Whether an entity is placed into `watchlist`, `restricted`, or `blocked` is determined through subsequent evidence submission, community review, voting, and appeals.

In other words, this document answers what kinds of risk fall within AntiGEO's concern; the status process answers what level of risk is supported by the available record.

### 8. Non-Goals

AntiGEO does not currently aim to do the following:

- Make value judgments about product quality or merit
- Determine the truth or falsity of a single item of content
- Replace legal or regulatory conclusions
- Provide a unified standard for ranking search result quality
- Directly require any specific enforcement strategy from downstream systems

Downstream systems may reference the AntiGEO registry, but whether and how to apply it, and with what level of enforcement, remains their own decision.

This definition document may be revised in the future through community governance.
