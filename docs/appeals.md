# Appeals / 申诉与复审

[中文](#中文) | [English](#english)

## 中文

### 1. 文档目的

本文件用于定义 AntiGEO 中申诉与复审的基本规则、适用场景、处理流程和结果类型。它说明谁可以发起申诉或复审、在什么条件下可以受理，以及相关结果如何影响注册表状态。

### 2. 申诉与复审的作用

申诉与复审的主要作用在于纠正错误、补充上下文、提交反证、更新事实基础。它们不是自动推翻既有记录的快捷通道，也不应被视为绕过正常审核与投票的替代程序。

申诉与复审处理的是注册表状态及其证据基础，而不是对现实世界主体作终局裁定。

### 3. 谁可以发起申诉或复审

以下主体原则上可以发起申诉或复审：

- 被记录对象自身或其代表
- 社区成员
- 能提供补充材料、事实更正或反证的参与者

发起申诉或复审并不代表其主张自动成立。对于明显恶意、重复、骚扰式或证据不足的申诉，社区可以拒绝受理、暂缓处理或直接关闭。

### 4. 什么情况下可以发起申诉或复审

申诉或复审通常可以在以下情形下发起：

- 原提案或原状态依据的事实有误
- 有新的可复核证据
- 有重要上下文此前缺失
- 有明确反证
- 原有证据失效、被误解或被证明不可靠
- 对现有状态与证据强度匹配关系存在合理争议

是否受理，不取决于申诉方身份本身，而取决于其是否提出了足以支持重新审查的理由与材料。

### 5. 申诉或复审应包含的基本内容

申诉或复审请求至少应尽量包含以下内容：

- 涉及的对象
- 当前状态
- 申诉或复审类型
- 支持理由
- 新证据、反证或事实更正
- 必要的上下文说明
- 期望结果，例如维持、降级、移除或重新审查

仅重复已有观点而无新增材料，通常不足以启动正式复审。主观不满、品牌立场或对结果本身的不喜欢，不能单独构成充分依据。

### 6. 基本流程

申诉与复审的一般流程如下：

1. 提交申诉或复审请求。
2. 初步检查完整性与可受理性。
3. 审核新增材料、反证或更正内容。
4. 视情况决定是否进入正式复审或投票。
5. 形成结论并记录结果。
6. 保留必要历史，避免覆盖既有记录。

初步检查主要关注材料是否完整、理由是否明确、证据是否可复核，以及请求是否属于 AntiGEO 的处理范围。

### 7. 可能的结果类型

申诉或复审的结果类型通常可以包括：

- 维持原状态
- 降级状态
- 升级状态
- 从注册表中移除
- 更正文档性或元数据错误
- 要求补充材料后再审

结果应尽量以公开、可审计的方式记录，并保留必要的处理历史。

### 8. 处理申诉与复审时应遵循的原则

社区在处理申诉与复审时，应遵循以下原则：

- 证据优先
- 谨慎认定
- 不自动推翻既有结论
- 对新增材料保持开放
- 对反证和上下文更正给予合理重视
- 状态调整应与证据强度匹配
- 避免情绪化、竞争性或骚扰式利用申诉机制

新的申诉或复审不自动推翻旧结果。状态是否调整，仍应取决于新增材料是否足以改变既有证据基础或风险判断。

### 9. 不受理或中止处理的情况

在以下常见情形下，社区可以不受理、中止处理或要求补充后再行提交：

- 明显缺乏新增材料
- 重复提交且无实质新增内容
- 存在骚扰、恶意或滥用迹象
- 关键事实仍完全无法复核
- 请求内容明显超出 AntiGEO 范围
- 仅要求“因为不喜欢结果所以撤销”

申诉机制的目的在于修正和更新记录，而不是制造持续性干扰、竞争性攻击或程序性拖延。

### 10. 与其它文档的关系

各文档分工如下：

- `definition.md`：负责界定范围
- `governance.md`：负责总体治理框架
- `evidence-policy.md`：负责证据规范
- `voting.md`：负责正式投票规则
- `appeals.md`：负责申诉与复审处理规则

当具体规则出现交叉时，应以 `governance.md` 作为总体治理框架，并结合本文件与对应专题文档共同理解。

### 11. 早期项目说明

AntiGEO 仍处于早期阶段，申诉与复审规则未来可能随着社区经验而通过公开方式修订。此类修订应保持透明、克制与可审计，并继续强调证据优先、谨慎认定与防止滥用。

## English

### 1. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the basic rules, applicable scenarios, process, and result types for appeals and re-review in AntiGEO. It explains who may initiate an appeal or re-review, under what conditions such a request may be accepted, and how the outcome may affect `watchlist`, `restricted`, and `blocked` in the registry.

### 2. The Function of Appeals and Re-Review

Appeals and re-review are intended to correct errors, add missing context, submit counter-evidence, or update the factual basis of a record. They are not a shortcut for automatically overturning an existing record, and they should not be treated as a substitute for ordinary review and voting.

Appeals and re-review address registry status and its evidentiary basis, not a final determination about a real-world entity.

### 3. Who May Initiate an Appeal or Re-Review

The following may in principle initiate an appeal or re-review:

- The recorded entity itself, or its representative
- Community members
- Participants able to provide supporting materials, factual corrections, or counter-evidence

Filing an appeal or re-review does not mean that the claim is automatically accepted. The community may decline, defer, or close requests that are plainly malicious, repetitive, harassing, or unsupported by sufficient material.

### 4. When an Appeal or Re-Review May Be Initiated

An appeal or re-review may generally be initiated in situations such as:

- The facts relied on in the original proposal or status are incorrect
- There is new verifiable evidence
- Important context was previously missing
- There is clear counter-evidence
- Previously relied-on evidence has become invalid, was misunderstood, or has been shown to be unreliable
- There is a reasonable dispute over whether the current status matches the strength of the record

Whether a request should be accepted depends not on the identity of the person submitting it, but on whether the request provides sufficient grounds and materials to justify revisiting the matter.

### 5. What an Appeal or Re-Review Should Include

An appeal or re-review request should include, at minimum where possible:

- The entity involved
- The current status
- The type of appeal or re-review
- Supporting grounds
- New evidence, counter-evidence, or factual corrections
- Necessary contextual explanation
- The requested outcome, such as maintain, upgrade, downgrade, remove, or re-examine

Simply repeating an existing position without new material will usually not be enough to trigger a formal re-review. Mere dissatisfaction, brand preference, or dislike of the outcome is not sufficient on its own.

### 6. Basic Process

The general process for appeals and re-review is as follows:

1. Submit an appeal or re-review request.
2. Conduct an admissibility and completeness check.
3. Review the new materials, counter-evidence, or factual corrections.
4. Decide whether the matter should proceed to formal re-review or voting.
5. Reach a conclusion and record the result.
6. Preserve history rather than overwrite records.

The initial check is mainly concerned with whether the submission is complete, whether the grounds are clear, whether the evidence is verifiable, and whether the request falls within AntiGEO's scope.

### 7. Possible Result Types

Possible outcomes of appeals and re-review may include:

- Maintain the current status
- Downgrade the status
- Upgrade the status
- Remove the entity from the registry
- Correct documentary or metadata errors
- Request additional materials before further review

Outcomes should be recorded openly and in an auditable manner to the extent practical, with the necessary history preserved.

### 8. Principles for Handling Appeals and Re-Review

The community should follow these principles when handling appeals and re-review:

- Evidence first
- Cautious determination
- Do not automatically overturn existing conclusions
- Remain open to new materials
- Give reasonable weight to counter-evidence and contextual correction
- Status changes should match evidence strength
- Avoid emotional, competitive, or harassing use of the appeals process

A new appeal or re-review does not automatically override an earlier result. Whether the status should change still depends on whether the new material is sufficient to change the evidentiary basis or risk assessment.

### 9. When a Request May Be Declined or Suspended

The community may decline, suspend, or require supplementation in situations such as:

- There is an obvious lack of new material
- The submission is repetitive and adds no substantive new content
- There are signs of harassment, malice, or abuse
- Key facts remain entirely unverifiable
- The request is clearly outside the AntiGEO scope
- The request is effectively only that the result should be withdrawn because it is disliked

The purpose of the appeals process is to correct and update the record, not to enable persistent disruption, competitive attacks, or procedural delay.

### 10. Relationship to Other Documents

The documents are divided as follows:

- `definition.md`: defines scope
- `governance.md`: provides the overall governance framework
- `evidence-policy.md`: defines evidence standards
- `voting.md`: defines the rules for formal voting
- `appeals.md`: defines the rules for appeals and re-review

Where rules overlap, `governance.md` should be treated as the overall governance framework and read together with this document and the relevant topic-specific document.

### 11. Early-Stage Project Note

AntiGEO is still at an early stage, and the rules for appeals and re-review may be revised publicly as the community gains practical experience. Such revisions should remain transparent, restrained, and auditable, while continuing to emphasize evidence-first review, cautious determination, and abuse prevention.

These appeals and re-review rules may be revised in the future through an open community process.
